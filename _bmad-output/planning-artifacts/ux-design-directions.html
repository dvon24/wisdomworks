WisdomWorks Design Directions

Design Directions

Six visual directions for WisdomWorks, all sharing the core layout: globe centered, chat sidebar, morning briefing from sidebar with inline chat. Each explores a different balance of immersion, information density, and control surface placement.

Logo Concept

The WisdomWorks mark: a stylized "W" formed by connected nodes and arcs within a globe silhouette. The nodes represent organizational knowledge points; the arcs represent the intelligence connections WisdomWorks discovers. The "W" emerges from the network — the brand is the connections.

Recommended

1. Command Deck

Globe centered with right sidebar for chat + briefing. Neumorphic controls orbit the globe bottom/sides. Briefing cards stack in sidebar with inline decision buttons. Clean, balanced, functional.

2. Immersive Cinema

Maximizes globe presence — larger globe, narrower sidebar. Minimal controls visible. Cinematic background most visible. Briefing slides in as an expanded sidebar state. Dramatic first impression.

3. Mission Control

Instrument panel across the bottom with real-time metrics. Wider sidebar with status indicators. Data-forward approach — more visible metrics, health indicators, connection counts.

4. Split Horizon

Wider left sidebar (40%) with chat + briefing. Globe takes right 60%. Sidebar is the primary work surface — reading, deciding, chatting. Globe provides context alongside.

5. Floating Panels

Globe fills entire viewport. Sidebar collapsed to icon rail on right. Chat + briefing panel slides out on demand. Maximum immersion — the globe IS the interface until you need content.

6. Cockpit HUD

HUD-style data overlays directly on the globe surface. Real-time stats float as transparent overlays. Right sidebar for chat. Military/aerospace feel — pure information cockpit.

Direction 3: Mission Control

Data-forward cockpit. Neumorphic instrument cards along the bottom display real-time metrics — connection counts, active agents, silo warnings, time saved, health score. Wider sidebar (400px) with four tabs including dedicated Agents and Alerts views. Priority badges on briefing cards. The most information-dense direction.

  • Controls: Top toggle + zoom levels, bottom instrument panel
  • Briefing: Priority-tagged cards with status badges
  • Density: High — mission-critical data always visible

Direction 5: Floating Panels

Maximum immersion. The globe fills the entire viewport at 560px — the largest of any direction. A collapsed icon rail (60px) on the right edge provides navigation. Clicking an icon slides out a glassmorphic panel (360px) alongside the rail. The globe is never obscured by the sidebar — it shifts left to accommodate. When panels are collapsed, it's pure globe + cinematic background. The most dramatic "first impression" direction.

  • Globe: Full viewport, 560px, shifts when panel opens
  • Sidebar: 60px icon rail + 360px slide-out panel
  • Controls: Minimal toggle at bottom, zoom via icon rail
  • Briefing: Conversational inline with chat, compact
  • Density: Minimal — pure immersion, content on demand

Direction 6: Cockpit HUD

Heads-up display aesthetic. Real-time metrics float as transparent overlays directly on the globe surface — connections, signal health, active agents, silo count. No instrument panel cards; the data lives ON the visualization. Full control bar at bottom. Right sidebar for chat and briefing. Military/aerospace feel — Devon IS in the cockpit.

  • Globe: Center, 420px, with HUD data overlays
  • Sidebar: 380px right, tabbed (Briefing / Chat / Activity)
  • Controls: Full bottom bar with zoom levels, toggle, settings
  • Data: HUD overlays on globe surface — transparent, always visible
  • Density: High — data-on-glass approach
